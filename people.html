The Research Team

Group Leader

Dr. Robert Seaborne

Robert completed his PhD (Liverpool John Moores University and Keele Medical School) in molecular physiology in 2018, studying the role and dynamics of DNA methylation in human and rodent muscle during periods of growth and loss. He transitioned to the Rakyan Lab (QMUL, London) to work on understanding the consequence of epigenetic/genetic interactions at the ribosomal DNA locus before obtaining Lundbeck Foundation funding to pursue a 2-year fellowship with Dr. Ochala’s group at the University of Copenhagen. In 2023, he moved back to London and established his own research lab at King’s College London.

Dr Daniel Vaughan

Postdoc in computational muscle biology. Jan 2026 - present. Daniel completed his PhD at the University of Nottingham, jointly between the Schools of Mathematical Sciences and Pharmacy, where he developed continuum mechanics–based models to study how cells respond to fluctuating environments. His research focused on phase-field modelling and high-performance computing to simulate complex cellular dynamics. He also holds a Master’s degree in Mathematics from the University of Nottingham. In February 2026, he joined the Seaborne Lab, where he works on the analysis of MuscleOmics data and the development of computational algorithms for its processing.
